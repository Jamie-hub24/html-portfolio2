My Hobbies

Coding

I find coding to be both a logical puzzle and a creative outlet. Whether it's building web applications, designing game mechanics, or experimenting with new programming languages, I love the process of turning an idea into something tangible. Right now, I'm focusing on C# and Unity game development and full-stack web development, blending technical skills with creative problem-solving.

Reading & Creative Writing

Reading has always been a gateway to new worlds for me. I love how literature can shift your perspective, change your mood, and transport you somewhere entirely new. Whether it's classic novels, modern fiction, or books on the craft of writing, I find immense joy in exploring different styles and ideas.

Alongside reading, I'm deeply passionate about creative writing. I enjoy experimenting with different storytelling techniques, always looking for new ways to bring characters and worlds to life. I've written short stories, and I'm currently working on a children's novel, weaving together themes that resonate with young readers while building toward a grander narrative. Gaming

I love games that tell deep, engaging stories. Narrative-driven RPGs are my favourites, and I'm fascinated by how games can create immersive worlds and emotional experiences. This passion for storytelling in games has naturally led me to explore narrative design and game development.

My favourite games include the Life Is Strange series, The Last of Us Parts 1 & 2, Mass Effect, Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic 1 & 2, the Dragon Age series, The Witcher 3, Night In The Woods (which I think is possibly the best written game ever), and the Telltale Walking Dead series.

Music

Music has always been a huge part of my life. I play the guitar, and I'm drawn to genres that have depth and atmosphere, like post-rock and classic rock. Whether it's listening to expansive, cinematic soundscapes or playing my own riffs, music is something that inspires me and helps me think creatively.

Philosophy

I enjoy philosophy because it challenges me to think critically about the world and my place in it. Whether it's discussing ethics, free will, or existentialism, I find that philosophy provides a great way to engage with big ideas and question assumptions.

Art

I've always been drawn to art that captures movement, emotion, and light. The Impressionists are a favourite of mine, as their work feels alive and expressive. I also appreciate modern art, especially pieces that challenge conventional ideas and explore new ways of seeing the world.

Film and Film Making

Film is a medium that combines storytelling, visual composition, sound design, and more into a powerful experience. I enjoy films that transport me to different worlds and offer new perspectives. Over the years, I've explored filmmaking as a way to tell stories and experiment with visual techniques. I've taken several filmmaking and photography courses, including an intensive weekend course at Ealing Studios, gaining hands-on experience in bringing ideas to life.

Gardening

There's something incredibly grounding about working with plants. It is an ongoing process of learning about nature and enjoying it's natural rhythms and cycles. My garden is an evolving experiment, and I enjoy figuring out which plants will thrive in my alkaline soil - which can be a major challenge! Recently, I've been working on a rockery project, finding ways to create a visually appealing and sustainable garden without breaking the bank.
